Leave Your Mark on Mars (via NASA)

Aug 20th, 2015

NASA announced it will be accepting names to board the InSight spacecraft on its upcoming mission to Mars.

In 1977 NASA sent its first time capsule of sorts into deep space. Aboard Voyager 1 and Voyager 2 are discs made of gold that hold images and sounds from Earth. While Voyager 2 is now in the outermost layer of the heliosphere, Voyager 1 has left our solar system and is now in interstellar space.

If you missed the opportunity back in '77 or if you weren't around at the time like myself, now is your chance to leave your mark in space all without taking a day off work.

NASA will be sending a silicon microchip containing the names of those wanting to take part. In March of 2016, the spacecraft InSight will launch from California carrying the names. Insight will make its 6 month long journey to Mars, landing at a point fittingly called "Elysium Planitia, (Plain of Ideal Happiness)".
